Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our pageant, application process, and organization.

Applicants must be South African citizens, identify as female, be between the ages of 18 and 35, and have a medically documented disability. We welcome women of all disabilities, both visible and non-visible.

Contestants are judged on more than just beauty. Key criteria include their passion for advocacy, community involvement, leadership potential, and their ability to articulate their vision for a more inclusive South Africa. The competition includes interviews, a talent portion, and an evening wear presentation.

No, there is no fee to apply for the pageant. We aim to make this opportunity accessible to all eligible women.

Finalists will be expected to attend workshops and pre-pageant events. The winner will serve as an ambassador for Miss South Africa Disability for one year, a role which includes public speaking engagements, media appearances, and championing our advocacy campaigns. We work with the winner to ensure commitments are manageable and accommodating of her needs.

We provide mentorship, leadership training, and workshops on public speaking and advocacy for all our finalists. We are also committed to ensuring all our events and venues are fully accessible.

Empowering women with disabilities through advocacy, community, and leadership.
